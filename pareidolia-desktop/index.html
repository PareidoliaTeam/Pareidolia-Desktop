Labels

?

Labels are the categories your model will learn to recognize.

Each label should represent a distinct class — for example, "cat" or "dog".

Add at least two labels before training. Click a label to assign datasets to it.

Training Summary

?

Progress Bar: Fills up as each epoch completes. The bar may stay at 100% for a moment while the training process finalizes records and converts the model — this is normal.

Final Accuracy: How often the model predicted correctly on training data. Higher is better.

Final Loss: How wrong the model's predictions were during training. Lower is better.

Val Accuracy: Accuracy measured on unseen validation data — a more honest measure of real-world performance.

Val Loss: Loss on validation data. If this rises while training loss falls, the model may be overfitting.

Training Mode
?

Use Pretrained Model — Recommended for most users. Starts from a model already trained on millions of images, so it learns your labels faster and with less data.

Start From Scratch — Builds the model from random weights. Best for experienced developers who want full control over architecture and training.

Epochs
?

An epoch is one full pass through your training data.

More epochs = more training, but too many can cause overfitting — where the model memorizes your training images instead of learning general patterns, making it perform poorly on new images.

A good starting point is 10–20 epochs. Watch the Val Loss: if it starts rising while training loss keeps falling, set it lower.
